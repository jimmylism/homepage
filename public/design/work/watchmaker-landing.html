Our Story

Born from a passion for the immeasurable

In 1891, Édouard Vaucher established his atelier on the Rue de la Paix in Geneva with a single conviction: that the measurement of time was not a mechanical problem, but an artistic calling.

Over thirteen decades, that conviction has been passed from hand to hand — father to son, master to apprentice — becoming the invisible thread that binds every watch we create to the one that preceded it.

Henri Vaucher IV Maître Horloger, Fourth Generation

In-House Manufacture

Every component — from the mainspring to the escapement wheel — is designed and produced within our Geneva atelier.

II

All Vaucher movements carry the prestigious Poinçon de Genève, the strictest quality hallmark in haute horlogerie.

Lifetime Service

Every timepiece we produce is accompanied by a lifetime service commitment, ensuring it endures across generations.

The Atelier

Three thousand hours to create one

01
Design & Engineering

Each new reference begins with months of drawings, tolerances calculated to the nearest micron, and digital models refined until every proportion is resolved.

02
Component Manufacture

Our machinists produce over 300 individual components per movement using CNC equipment calibrated daily to sub-micron accuracy.

03
Anglage & Finishing

Every visible surface receives hand-bevelling, polishing, or perlage — work that can take a single watchmaker an entire week for one movement plate.

04
Assembly & Regulation

A master watchmaker assembles and regulates each movement by hand, testing it across six positions over a minimum of 16 days before certification.
